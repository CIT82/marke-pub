Park Info

General Park Information

Rocky Mountain National Park, located in north-central Colorado, is a pristine expanse of natural beauty encompassing approximately 265,461 acres. ￼ Established in 1915, the park showcases the grandeur of the Rocky Mountains, featuring elevations ranging from 7,860 to 14,259 feet, with Longs Peak being the highest summit.

The Continental Divide runs through the heart of the park, influencing its diverse ecosystems. Visitors can explore lush valleys, dense forests, alpine tundra, and over 150 pristine lakes. Trail Ridge Road, the highest paved through-road in the United States, traverses the park, reaching elevations up to 12,183 feet and offering breathtaking panoramic views.
