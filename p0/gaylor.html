Hike Info

Description

The Gaylor Lakes Trail in Yosemite National Park offers a moderately challenging hike that rewards adventurers with stunning alpine lake vistas and expansive mountain views.

The trail head for this hike is right next to the eastern gate to Yosemite, and across the road from the Mt. Dana trail head.

What I Love About This Hike

This one is short but steep you gain about 600 feet in less than a mile, but you are soon rewarded with fantastic views of Mt. Dana and Mt. Gibb.

The lower lake offers expansive view back towards the Cathedral range, and the upper offers a chance to explore an abandoned mine shaft.
