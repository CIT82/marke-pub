Hike Info

Description

Mt. Dana is the second highest peak in Yosemite National Peak, Mt. Lyell is the highest. It is located at the eastern edge of the park.

One thing will become clear when you embark on this hike, you are at high altitude. You start at Tioga Pass nearly 10,000 feet in elevation and ascend to 13,060 at the summit. You will most likely feel the effects of the elevation. I know for myself around 11,000 feet I notice a marked difference in my exersion levels when I move. I would recommend taking a measured approach to climbing the mountain. And be on the look out for sign of "altitude sickness". If you feel symptoms it is best to start making you way back down.

It is worth noting that much of this hike is above timberline and very exposed. Care should be taken to protect yourself from the sun and bring plenty of water. And as always be mindful of changing weather conditions as thunderstorms can form quickly in the High Sierra

What I Love About This Hike

The beginning of the hike starts gradually along the edge Dana meadow with some ponds and wild flowers. I start this hike early in the morning and this area is remarkably peaceful and calm in the early morning when the sun has not broken the crest of the Sierra.

The views from the top are simply amazing with views surrounding mountains and down into Owens Valley and Mono Lake.
